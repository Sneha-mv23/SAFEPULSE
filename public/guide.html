Trusted Safety Guide

Essential tips for staying safe in high-risk zones

Location Awareness

  • Always keep your GPS/location on while traveling.
  • Share your live location with a trusted contact.
  • Be aware of red zones or high-alert areas on the map.

In Case of Emergency

  • Use the SafePulse Emergency button to alert others nearby.
  • Call 112 or 100 immediately if you feel threatened.
  • Stay calm and avoid running or causing panic.

Stay Alert

  • Report any suspicious activity or unattended objects to authorities.
  • Avoid crowded or isolated areas during high-alert periods.
  • Follow official advisories from police or local government.

Digital Safety

  • Keep your phone charged at all times.
  • Use encrypted messaging apps when needed.
  • Disable auto-sync and Bluetooth when not in use.

Travel & Transportation

  • Use verified taxi services or ride-sharing apps with driver tracking.
  • Avoid traveling alone late at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Check local transport advisories before departing.

Communication Tips

  • Keep emergency contact numbers saved and accessible.
  • Send periodic updates to friends or family while in risky areas.
  • Keep a backup phone or power bank for long travels.

Mental Preparedness

  • Stay informed but avoid constant exposure to distressing news.
  • Practice breathing or calming techniques during stressful events.
  • Have a personal safety plan and rehearse basic emergency responses.

Emergency Kit Essentials

  • Carry ID, water, snacks, a flashlight, and a basic first-aid kit.
  • Include any required medications and a list of allergies.
  • Have some cash and backup documents in a secure place.
